About me

Lynwood Savage, (61) the Pennsylvania Convention Center Executive Safety Manager is the endorsed democratic candidate for state representative in the 192nd legislative district serving Overbrook, Carroll Park West Philly and Wynnefield. He is born and raised in the Overbrook section of West Philadelphia and is the only graduate of the mighty Overbrook High School in the race. He is best known for his 38 years of tireless service to the Democratic Party serving as a local and state wide democratic committeeman. Moreover, he is responsible for getting countless democratic politicians elected; Including President Bill Clinton, President Barack Obama, Congressman Robert Brady, State Senator Vincent Hughes, Mayor Ed Rendell and State Representative Louise Bishop to name a few. As a Congressional Liaison he navigated tough political waters and helped to secure funding for the Delaware River Channel dredging project designed to deepen the river’s shipping channel from 40 to 45 feet from Philadelphia/Camden to the Atlantic. Our ports are responsible for 75,000 jobs and $1billion in wages for our region.
Community safety has always been a passion of his, having served as Director of the Fugitive Warrant Unit in the Philadelphia Sheriff’s Department. Understanding that you cannot arrest your way out of the problem of crime for years Lynwood volunteered with Men United for a Better Philadelphia, an organization that focused on decreasing gun violence through engaging warring street gangs and assisting them with conflict resolution and finding job opportunities. In addition, Lynwood is a long time member of NOBLE (The National Organization of Black Law Enforcement Executives) Lynwood’s service to our neighborhoods is stellar and his commitment to public education is genuine. Whether helping seniors in Overbrook, Wynnefield and Carroll Park or as the lead NTA at Beeber Middle School, he has always been grounded in the community. Lynwood is dedicated to assisting small business incubators having been a small businessman who once owned Savage Sandwiches, best known for their delicious hot sausages. Later he became a proprietor of an AT&T franchise. A proud member of Mt. Carmel Baptist Church this family man is blessed with a beautiful wife and six lovely children and three grandchildren.

Housing and Economic Development

There are areas in my district that are in dire straits with respect to housing and business development. Houses and storefronts stay vacant not days, weeks, even months – but decades. My office will not only direct state resources to the district to address these problems, but we will help organize and support, where necessary, competent, proactive community development organizations.
We also must admit that the available, conventional models for helping to start new businesses have been ineffective. We have to be more creative and convince funding sources to grant money to potential entrepreneurs. We have to advocate for funding resources based on an individuals’ desire and experience, not just their credit score.

Senior Services

My office will maintain a Senior Services Liaison who will provide dedicated service to district seniors who need assistance in all aspects of their life. Support will be provided in reviewing insurance options, addressing housing concerns, writing wills, consumer protection and obtaining rent rebates.

Criminal Justice and the Returning Citizens

Profit has no place in some fields of endeavor. I will work as hard as possible to rid the state of private prisons. Moreover, I will direct resources to programs that enhance workforce preparation during incarceration, improve placement services and remove barriers to employment for ex-offenders.

education

Schools are our most important community based institutions and therefore, I am committed to funding public education for all students, their parents, teachers and administrators in Philadelphia and throughout Pennsylvania. In Philadelphia, teacher shortages and the lack of substitutes have been at critical levels. This situation has been worsened as a result of outsourcing the provision of qualified substitutes in at risk schools. I will work to reverse this pattern so that the Philadelphia School District once again manages the substitute teacher process. The work needed to turn around schools does not only happen in the halls of power in Harrisburg or City Hall. Effective service from my office will require building working relationships with schools. It is in this spirit that my staff and I will visit our schools on a routine basis (at least monthly), so we can listen and stay versed on the local problems affecting them. Finally, I am committed to supporting struggling HBCU’s in the state (Historically Black Colleges & Universities) like Cheyney University and Lincoln University, and help them weather their economic challenges.
